Judo

The beautiful gentle art...of destruction

Judo was created in 1882 by Kano Jigoro Shihan. As an educational method derived from the ancient Japanese Samurai art of Jujutsu, Judo became an official Olympic sport in 1964. Judo is a highly codified sport in which the mind controls the expression of the body and is a sport which contributes to educating & "in-forming" individuals.

Beyond competitions and combat, Judo involves technical research, practice of katas, self-defense work, physical preparation and sharpening of spirit.

As a discipline derived from ancestral traditions, judo was designed by Shihan Kano to embrace the strengths of a pedagogical education system.

Judo History

Judo was synthesised from grappling techniques of the ancient art of Jujutsu by Jigaro Kano in 1882

As a progressive educator, he developed a clear syllabus and a belt-ranking system to take the art into the modern era

Today, it is an Olympic Sport, and is one of the most popular sports globally

World Judo Day is celebrated on 28 October, and celebrates the Judo ethos of respect, friendship and solidarity of the Human Family

Techniques

Judo is primarily a grappling style

It seeks to unbalance, throw and pin an adversary, using whole body mechanics

A skilled practitioner can vary the level of lethality of the techniques, but it is generally geared towards non-lethal applications

A majority of law enforcement agencies around the world tend to adopt Judo as part of their unarmed syllabus

As a sport, some aspects of Judo has been modified to emphasise safety, and is thus often regarded as a "Gentle Art"

Philosophy

One of the main principles of Judo is "Maximum efficiecy, Minimum effort"

As a science educator, Kano focused on using science to express the techniques of Judo: Force, Fulcrum, Lever

Thus a smaller, lighter person can still prevail over a larger assailant, and great for women and children

Judo's foundations focus on initiative & leadership, courtesy & respect, gentleness & kindness
