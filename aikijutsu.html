Aikijutsu

The Art which all others come from

Aikijujutsu, also known as jiu-Jitsu and ju-jitsu, is a family of Japanese martial arts and a system of close combat (unarmed or with a minor weapon) that can be used in a defensive or offensive manner to kill or subdue one or more weaponless or armed and armored opponents.

A subset of techniques from certain styles of jujutsu were used to develop modern martial arts and combat sports, such as judo, kendo, sambo, aikido, Brazilian jiu-jitsu, and mixed martial arts.

Jujutsu

Jujutsu refers to the family of Samurai Martial Arts that was used in the battlefield during feudal Japan

It encompasses a wide range of weapons and techniques: Sword, Knife, Staff, Halberd, Kicks, Punches, Throws, Locks, Chokes

Almost every Japanese martial art such as Kendo, Aikido, Judo, Kempo traces its roots to Jujutsu

The word "Aiki" that is used as a prefix to Jujutsu denotes that the art focuses on circular, "soft" techniques that blend and confuse the enemy

The terms Jujutsu, Aikijutsu, Aiki-Jujutsu generally refer to the Arts that descent from Samurai battlefield techniques

Students of the Art progress from unarmed techniques to weapons such as the Japanese Katana Sword

Unarmed Techniques

Comprises strikes, kicks, throws, joint locks, pins, chokes that were used against armed & armoured Samurai

The Art focuses on using body mechanics and the momentum of the assailant to gain an advantage

Jujutsu Joint locks, pins and chokes are often seen in modern close quarter police or military fighting arts

Footwork, blending energies, managing line of intent and devastating techniques are hallmarks of this Art

As a sport, some aspects of Judo has been modified to emphasise safety, ans thus is often regarded as a "Gentle Art"

Weapons

The feudal warrior class called "Samurai" honed their skills for survival on the battlefield

Many sword schools flourished during the Samural era; and alongside them, schools specialising in non-sword systems proliferated as well

A high level Jujutsu practitioner would also be skilled with weapons such as the Katana (Sword), (Naginata) Halberd, Bo (Staff), Knife (Wakizashi)

The emphasis is on the perfection of the technique, breathing, power-generation, focus and awareness
